Extra Information

Income *After Taxes

Money you receive regularly, such as wages, allowances, or side earnings. This is the starting point of your budget and determines how much you can spend or save.

Housing and Living Expenses

Costs related to where you live, such as rent, utilities, groceries, and household supplies. These are usually essential and recurring expenses.

Health

Money spent on medical needs like doctor visits, prescriptions, insurance, or personal care items. Planning for health expenses helps avoid financial stress.

Auto and Transportation

Expenses for getting around, including gas, car payments, insurance, maintenance, or public transportation. These costs can be fixed or variable.

Education

Spending related to school or learning, such as tuition, books, supplies, or course fees. This category helps track investment in your future.

Loans and Credit Cards

Payments made toward borrowed money, including student loans, personal loans, or credit card balances. Budgeting for these helps avoid debt buildup.

Savings and Investing

Money set aside for future goals, emergencies, or growth through investments. This category supports long-term financial stability.

Giving

Money donated to charities, religious organizations, or given as gifts. Including this in a budget ensures generosity is planned.

Misc.

Any expenses that don’t fit into other categories, such as entertainment or hobbies. This helps account for small or irregular spending.
